Origins of the Safe Behavioral DNA test

Safe Behavioral DNA was developed at the request of the Bolivar Risk Administrator (ARL) in Colombia, an institution that provides risk prevention tools to more than 1 million people in Colombia.

The Safe Behavioral DNA workplace safety test was developed and corroborated by extensive scientific research aimed at identifying the traits that differentiate people who are injured at work from those who are not. Designing a solid and reliable workplace safety test to allow companies to predict safe behavior.

For its creation, first, an extensive bibliographic review of the individual factors associated with work related accidents was carried out, developing a preliminary questionnaire applied to a thousand workers belonging to 11 companies from different sectors, such as construction, mining, transportation, manufacture, services, among others.

The sample of one thousand workers considered two groups, 600 employees who had suffered documented work related accidents in the last two years and 400 who didn’t experienced any accidents at all in the same period. Both groups belonging to the same positions and selected companies.

A questionnaire with 136 questions with the goal of knowing what differentiated both groups. In the end getting a reliability rate of 0.93 and having identified significant differences between the two. The constructed model was based on extensive scientific research, identifying four factors that differentiated both groups of employees in terms of their accident history, observance of traffic rules, Self-control, Good Judgment - Risk Aversion and Attentiveness.

These four factors are the foundations when differentiating people according to their safe work behavior in companies.
